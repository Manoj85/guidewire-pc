## INSURANCE LINES — KNOW THESE COLD

Choks may probe your P&C depth. Be able to speak to each line of business you've touched.

### Workers' Compensation

- Insurance covering employees injured on the job — medical expenses, lost wages, disability, rehabilitation.
- **Benefits to employee:** medical treatment, wage replacement (typically ~66% of wages), disability payments, vocational rehab.
- **Benefits to employer:** protection from employee lawsuits related to workplace injuries.
- Mandatory in most U.S. states. Regulated at state level — each state has its own rules, benefit schedules, and fee schedules.
- **Your CompSource context:** CompSource Mutual is an Oklahoma-based workers' comp specialist. Single line of business — everything in their Guidewire footprint is workers' comp. This makes the upgrade complex because their configuration is deeply specialized for OK workers' comp regulatory requirements.
- Key Guidewire touchpoints: ClaimCenter (injury claims workflow, medical bill processing, reserves), PolicyCenter (workers' comp policy issuance), BillingCenter (premium billing, audit).

---

### Personal Lines

- Insurance sold to individuals and families — as opposed to businesses.
- Primary lines: personal auto, homeowners, renters, umbrella, personal watercraft.
- **Your Nationwide context:** Nationwide Personal Lines Transformation — PolicyCenter and BillingCenter for personal auto and personal property.
- High transaction volume, relatively standardized products. Rating is complex but products are well-defined.
- Key dynamics: price competition, digital distribution (direct-to-consumer), high regulatory change frequency, telematics/usage-based insurance emerging.

---

### Commercial Lines

- Insurance sold to businesses.
- Primary lines: commercial auto, commercial property, general liability, workers' comp (when sold as part of commercial package), BOP (Business Owners Policy), professional liability (E&O, D&O), specialty.
- More complex underwriting than personal lines — each risk is individually assessed.
- **Your EY context:** Several EY clients were commercial lines or mixed P&C carriers.
- Key dynamics: manuscript policies (customized for large commercial risks), longer underwriting cycles, broker distribution, complex reinsurance.
