## THINGS TO WATCH OUT FOR / POTENTIAL TOUGH QUESTIONS

**"You're coming from EY — a competitor. How do we know you won't bring EY's approach and culture and try to change things here?"**

> "Fair question. What I'm bringing is delivery discipline, Guidewire depth, and a P&C client network — not a consulting firm playbook. I've competed against VM and I've respected what you've built. The reason I'm here is because I want to operate in an environment where insurance is the whole business, not a vertical. I intend to learn VM's model, not import EY's."

**"We're a smaller firm than EY — resources are tighter. How do you adapt?"**

> "I've always run lean. Even at EY my philosophy was: the right team for the work, not the biggest team. Smaller firm means faster decisions, more direct client access, and more accountability — those are things I value. Tighter resources just means better prioritization, which I'd argue is a discipline, not a limitation."

**"Guidewire Select partner tier vs. Premier — does that concern you?"**

> "Partnership tier reflects scale of transactions, not quality of delivery. VM has six Guidewire specializations and a track record across 75+ carriers. I'd match that delivery depth against any Premier partner. The tier will grow as the practice scales — and I'd like to be part of that growth."
